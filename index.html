Oil Palm (Elaeis guineensis) FFB Yield Curve Modeling

In oil palm plantations across Malaysia, harvesting Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) at absolute chemical maturity maximizes oil extraction rates (OER). To model the development of an FFB from anthesis (flowering) to physiological ripeness (approx. 20 weeks), agrotechnologists use a Sigmoidal Growth Model (Logistic Curve). By studying the first derivative f'(t) of this growth curve, we can isolate the exact period of fastest mass accumulation and define optimal harvest windows.

L Asymptotic max bunch weight (carrying capacity in kg).
k Intrinsic growth rate constant (steepness index).
t₀ (weeks) Inflection point. Week of maximum physical growth, where f''(t) = 0.
